## Discussion Summary

### Topic: Hackathon Duration and Format
- **Discussion:** The team explored various timeframes to maximize participant engagement and feasibility. Proposals included a 12-hour sprint for focused coordination and a 24-hour immersive experience.
- **Outcome:** A consensus was reached on a **26-hour hackathon**. This duration accommodates a comprehensive "Day 0" for exploration and research, followed by a full day of development, workshops, and customer validation, ensuring a balanced and productive experience.

### Topic: Problem Statement Strategy & Domain Categorization
- **Discussion:** Approaches ranged from providing pre-defined problems to allowing participants to source their own or The concept of exploring domains (e.g., Energy, Healthcare) was discussed to structure the challenge.
- **Outcome:** A hybrid schema was adopted. Participants will identify problems from their surroundings and open-source communities on Day 0. The "Day 1" development phase will utilize a domain-based categorization system where participants select a sector, with evaluation focusing on scenario-based adaptability and cross-domain innovation.

### Topic: Operational Framework & Logistics
- **Discussion:** The team outlined the scale, cost structure, and mandatory components. The need to align with college schedules and secure permissions was emphasized.
- **Outcome:** The event is planned for **100 participants** with an operational cost contribution of **Rs. 149 per person**. The schema is built on a "Permission-First" approach, where all logistical and promotional activities will commence immediately upon securing institutional approvals. Sponsorship outreach is aligned with this timeline to ensure financial viability.
